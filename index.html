About

A researcher in trustworthy systems.

I am a Graduate Research Assistant at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, working in the AI CyberSafe Innovations Lab under the supervision of Dr. Shuvalaxmi Dass. My research sits at the intersection of privacy-preserving machine learning, federated systems, and proactive cyber defense, with applications in healthcare and intelligent transportation.

My published and under-review work systematizes the privacy landscape of federated fine-tuning for healthcare large language models — characterizing adversary types, attack surfaces, and layered defenses across the full LLM lifecycle. I have also developed a blockchain-secured federated transformer framework for real-time object detection in intelligent transportation systems, and I am designing network Moving Target Defense evaluation testbeds combining SDN, game theory, and adversarial modeling.

Before graduate school, I spent over four years as a Data Engineer at Grameenphone (Telenor Group), Bangladesh’s largest telecom operator serving 80M+ subscribers. That experience gave me a deep grounding in distributed data systems at production scale — infrastructure expertise that directly informs how I reason about federated learning deployments today.

Research Themes

Four threads of inquiry.

01 / Theme

Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning

Systematizing attack surfaces and layered defenses in federated fine-tuning of clinical LLMs and medical imaging systems.

02 / Theme

System-Level ML Engineering

Building federated pipelines that handle non-IID data, modality heterogeneity, edge latency, and Byzantine participants.

03 / Theme

Proactive Cyber Defense

SDN-based Moving Target Defense, game-theoretic attacker modeling, and adversarial evaluation frameworks.

04 / Theme

Applied Cryptography

Cryptographic primitives for trustworthy ML — group signatures, BFT consensus, and information-hiding schemes.
